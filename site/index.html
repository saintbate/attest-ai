What Attest does
AI system inventory
Catalogs every model you wrap — name, framework, purpose, inference volume, error rate. The foundation for an Article 11 technical file.
Risk classification
Maps each system to EU AI Act Annex III categories. Rule-based, deterministic, auditable.
Art. 6 · Annex III
Inference logging
Every prediction logged with input metadata, output summary, confidence, and latency. Optional SHA-256 input hash + customer-supplied reference links each inference to a reproducible source — without Attest ever storing the underlying frame.
Art. 12
Drift detection
Two-sample statistical tests (KS, MMD, Mann-Whitney) over confidence, latency, and error-rate streams. Reports p-value plus effect size.
Art. 72
Annex IV documentation
Markdown Annex IV files and OSCAL-aligned JSON assessment results, generated from your runtime data. Fields you still need to fill in are flagged — not hidden.
Art. 11 · Annex IV
Compliance dashboard
Real-time compliance posture. Gap analysis showing exactly what's missing, mapped to specific Articles.
Webhook & email alerts
Slack, PagerDuty, or email notifications for drift events, risk changes, and deadline reminders.
10-year retention
Timestamped evidence logs persisted to SQLite or your own database, covering the mandatory 10-year retention window.
Art. 18
Built for construction AI
Safety AI on the jobsite is high-risk.
Construction safety AI typically enters the EU AI Act as either a safety component of critical infrastructure (Article 6(1), Annex I) or a worker-monitoring system (Annex III §4). Either route triggers mandatory documentation, logging, monitoring, and human oversight — enforced from 2 August 2026. The specific classification depends on deployment context; Attest runs the analysis per system.

Scope
Where Attest ends — and what still needs a human.

Attest produces

  • Inference logs with input metadata, output, confidence, latency Art. 12
  • Input evidence linking — hash + customer-supplied reference, built for camera-based AI Art. 12
  • Drift signals with p-value and effect size Art. 15 · 72
  • Annex III risk classification Art. 6
  • Annex IV technical documentation — Markdown + OSCAL Art. 11
  • 10-year evidence retention Art. 18

Attest does not produce

  • Risk management system — your compliance lead writes the SOP Art. 9
  • Data governance policy Art. 10
  • Human oversight design — we capture decisions, your team defines them Art. 14
  • Conformity assessment sign-off Art. 43
  • Declaration of conformity Art. 47

1 EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689): Art. 11 + Annex IV (technical documentation), Art. 12 (automatic logging), Art. 15 (accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity evidence), Art. 72 (post-market monitoring plan).

2 EU AI Act Article 99. High-risk non-compliance: up to €15M or 3% of global annual turnover — whichever is higher. Prohibited practices: up to €35M or 7%. Enforcement for high-risk begins 2 August 2026.
