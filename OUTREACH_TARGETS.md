## Regulatory Context

### Why Construction AI Is High-Risk Under the EU AI Act

The EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689) classifies AI systems across a risk spectrum. Construction AI systems frequently fall into the **high-risk** category under **Annex III**, which triggers the most burdensome compliance obligations:

| Annex III Category | Construction AI Application | Example Companies |
|---|---|---|
| **§4 — Employment & worker management** | Worker performance monitoring, safety behavior tracking, task allocation, automated scheduling | viAct, Buildots, Versatile, Inspectly360 |
| **§2 — Critical infrastructure** | AI managing construction on transport, energy, water, or digital infrastructure | nPlan, Urbint, Doxel (data centers) |
| **§1 — Biometrics** | Facial recognition for site access, worker identification | viAct (if biometric features enabled) |
| **Safety-critical AI systems** | Computer vision for PPE detection, hazard identification, crane safety, structural inspection | viAct, Versatile, DeepQual, Buildots, OpenSpace |

### Provider Obligations for High-Risk AI (Article 16+)

Any company **providing** (developing/placing on market) a high-risk AI system in the EU must:

1. **Risk management system** — continuous, iterative process covering entire AI lifecycle
2. **Data governance** — training, validation, and testing data requirements
3. **Technical documentation** — sufficient detail for authorities to assess compliance
4. **Record-keeping / logging** — automatic recording of events during operation
5. **Transparency** — instructions for use provided to deployers
6. **Human oversight** — designed to allow effective human control
7. **Accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity** — appropriate levels throughout lifecycle
8. **Conformity assessment** — completed before placing system on market
9. **EU database registration** — registered before deployment
10. **Post-market monitoring** — ongoing monitoring system in place
11. **Quality management system** — documented and maintained

### Enforcement Timeline

| Date | Milestone |
|---|---|
| Feb 2, 2025 | Prohibited AI practices enforceable |
| Aug 2, 2025 | GPAI model obligations + governance provisions |
| Feb 2, 2026 | EC guidelines on high-risk classification expected |
| **Aug 2, 2026** | **HIGH-RISK AI OBLIGATIONS ENFORCEABLE (Annex III)** |
| Aug 2, 2027 | Annex I high-risk systems (product safety legislation) |

> **Note:** The EU Digital Omnibus proposal may extend the Annex III deadline to December 2, 2027 — but this is NOT yet law. Do not assume the extension will pass.
